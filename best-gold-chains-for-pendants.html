Buying Guide

Best gold chains for a pendant

A pendant needs a chain that hangs straight, carries the weight, and fits the bail. Here's what to pick.

Reviewed June 2026Independent · no paid placements

Best picks: Franco and box chains hang straight and resist twisting, and a solid rope is a versatile strong option. Go 22–24″ so the pendant rests mid-chest, match the width to the pendant's weight, and check the bail fits the chain.

Styles that hang right

Franco and box chains are stiff and hang dead straight, so a pendant sits where it should instead of flipping. A solid rope is the versatile all-rounder. Avoid herringbone for anything heavy — it kinks.

Get the fit right

  • Length: 22–24″ rests a pendant mid-chest on most men — see the pendant length guide.
  • Strength & width: match a heavier pendant with a sturdier, slightly wider solid chain so it isn't under-built.
  • Bail fit: the chain has to pass through the pendant's bail (the top loop) — a thick chain won't fit a small bail.

Frequently asked questions

What chain is best for a heavy pendant?

A sturdy, slightly wider solid chain in a Franco, box, or solid rope style — strong enough to carry the weight and stiff enough to hang straight. Make sure the bail fits the chain width.

What length chain for a pendant?

22–24 inches suits most men, resting the pendant mid-chest. Size up about 2 inches for a larger frame.

Will any chain fit my pendant?

Only if the chain passes through the pendant's bail. Check the chain width against the bail opening — thick chains won't fit small bails.
