Buying Guide

How to buy a Franco chain

Sleek, dense, and pendant-friendly — the Franco is one of the most durable chains. Here's how to choose a good one.

Reviewed June 2026Independent · no paid placements

Key calls: the Franco's tight links make it great for pendants and very durable; choose solid 14k, and match the width to whether you'll wear it alone or carry a pendant.

What to look for in a Franco

The Franco's tightly woven V-shaped links make it stiff, sleek, and resistant to twisting — ideal for holding a pendant straight. The usual rules apply: go solid for durability and value, pick 14k for the everyday standard, and choose a width that supports any pendant you'll hang. It's also clean enough to wear on its own. Frequently asked questions

Is a Franco chain good for a pendant?

Yes — its tight, stiff construction makes it hang straight and resist twisting, which is ideal for a pendant.

Are Franco chains durable?

Very — the densely woven links make Franco one of the more durable styles, especially solid.

Franco or Cuban?

Franco for a sleeker, pendant-friendly chain; Cuban for a bolder, flatter statement. Both are durable when solid.
