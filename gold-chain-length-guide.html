Sizing

Men's gold chain length guide

The same chain looks completely different at 20 inches versus 24. Here's where each length actually sits, and how to pick yours.

Reviewed June 2026Independent · no paid placements

Quick guide: For most men, 20″ sits at the top of the chest and is the everyday default, while 22–24″ sits mid-chest and is the go-to for pendants. Size up about 2″ if you have a larger build, and shorter (18″) for a snug, higher look on a smaller frame.

Adjust for height and build

The table assumes an average build — shift it to fit you. A larger or taller frame carries length well, so sizing up about 2″ keeps the proportions right (a 24″ on a big frame can sit like a 22″ on an average one). A smaller frame is the reverse: a 20″ may sit lower than the chart suggests, so 18–20″ often looks best.

Match length to the look

  • Chain worn alone, clean look: 20–22″ keeps it visible above most collars.
  • With a pendant: 22–24″ lets the pendant rest mid-chest where it reads clearly.
  • Layering multiple chains: stagger lengths (e.g. 20″ + 24″) so each is visible.
  • Under a shirt / subtle: 20″ sits near the collar line.

How to measure

Use a soft tape or a string against your neck/chest to see where a given length would fall, or measure a chain you already own from end to end (including the clasp). When in doubt between two lengths, the longer one is usually the safer choice for men — it's easier to wear a touch long than too tight.

Frequently asked questions

What length gold chain is best for men?

20 inches is the most popular everyday length for the average build, sitting at the top of the chest. For a pendant, 22–24 inches is the usual choice so it rests mid-chest. Size up about 2 inches for a larger frame.

What size chain for a pendant?

22–24 inches is standard for a pendant on most men, placing it around the middle of the chest where it reads clearly. See our pendant length guide for details.

How do I measure my chain length?

Measure an existing chain end to end including the clasp, or hold a tape/string against your neck and chest to see where a given length would fall.
